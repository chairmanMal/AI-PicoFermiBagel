The Story of this App

Welcome to the world of Pico Fermi Bagel. This is a game my children and I were taught to play by a family friend when the boys were in Elementary School. Its a logic game that involves numbers and requires healthy doses of deductive reasoning, informed guessing, and often just a touch of luck to master. Some research has taught me that the game is no secret with others. Many have written and published games to play it well before this effort. It has even showed up in at least one book. The one I am familiar with is titled, "Family Math" by Jean Kerr Stenmark, Virginia Thompson, and Ruth Cossey. There are many references to the game, its history, and how to play it on the internet and I encourage anyone interested to seek them out. For my family and me, the game has remained a perennial favorite well beyond the elementary math years most say the game targets. So in an effort to stay technically current as my professional career has wound down, I took on the task of capturing our family's favorite game as a fun and challenging app in this brave new world of mobile computing. I took some liberties to enhance the game to both increase its challenge and to make it more fun. Though honestly, the "classic" mode is still plenty fun enough for most.

I invite you to sit down with the app on your favorite mobile phone (not available yet) or tablet and give it a whirl. Hopefully, the game will become as 'addictive' for you as my family found it.

The Basic Game

This is a guessing game in that your objective is to deduce, in the correct order, the series of numbers that the computer has randomly selected. For each game a new random selection is made. Any given game will have both a multi-digit number to guess and a set of numbers to select from. In the "Classic" game as I learned it, there is a 3 digit number with each digit being chosen from the integers between 0 and 9. One other "rule" is that in a valid guess, no digit can be repeated. So for instance, a valid guess would be "234" but not "233".

The way you will helped to figure out what the "secret" number the computer has chosen (I refer to this as the 'Target') is that on each guess, you will be provided some feedback on how the digits in your guess relate to the Target's digits. The feedback will come in the form of 1 of 3 possible responses you will get for each digit in your guess. For your guess, you will be told how many of your digits are:

             Pico - meaning you have a digit that is in the Target though it is not in the right position ..... think "position"... this is good news in that you know your close.

             Fermi - meaning you have a digit that is in the Target and it is in the right position !!!! .... think "firm" ... this is great news as this means you have a part of the solution in hand even if you may not know which digit(s) is/are "Fermi".

             Bagel1- meaning you have a digit that is simply not part of the Target ... think of the bagel shape signifying "a big Zero" ... this could be very valuable information because knowing a number that is definitely not in the Target can be quite valuable in helping you  determine which numbers are in the Target and maybe even in helping to identify their proper positions.


1- This is a slight deviation from the original game I learned in that I will tell you how many digits are "bagels" in your guess whereas the original game only declared an entire answer to be a "bagel" when no digits were a pico or a fermi. Both the traditional game and my version are giving you the same information though I spare you a little simple arithmetic.

A Sample "Classic" Game

So let's work thru a sample game. Say the computer generated Target is "385" and the possible set of digits are from 0 - 9. Let's follow a set of guesses, the feedback given, and some of the reasoning you might use to deduce the Target.
 Guess         Feedback                                  
 123            1 Pico, 0 Fermi, 2 Bagels

 At this point I know either the '1', the '2', or the '3' are in the Target and the other two numbers are not. I'll try to learn more and somewhat randomly choose the last digit to replace


 124             0 Pico, 0 Fermi, 3 Bagels    
    
 Excellent! My guess paid off, now I know '1','2', and '4' are not in the Target and that '3' is though not in the last spot. Let's move the '3' around and try to find the other digits of the Target

356              1 Pico, 1 Fermi, 1 Bagel 
          
 Hummm. The '3' could be the Fermi or it could be the Pico. Also, either the '5' or the '6' is in the Target but not both. I'm going to take the chance the '3' is the Fermi, which would then imply either the '5' or '6' needs to be in the other's position. I'll go with the '5' for no good reason other than it is chronologically after the '3'

 375              0 Pico, 2 Fermis, 1 Bagel   
       
  Ok, now I know I have the '3' and the '5' correct. I am sure the '7' isn't one of the Fermi's because, if it were, that would imply the '6' from the previous guess should have been a "Fermi" which it wasn't. Now I'm also sure the '3' is also one of the Fermi's, so on to replacing the '7' to find whether its position should be filled with the '8' or '9' given they are only two digits yet to be tried

 385  

                 !!!!! A WINNER in 5 guesses !!!!!

Hopefully you know get a sense for how the game is played.

The basic screen

The screen is as shown below. Each major section is identified and will be further described below.



Section A - the Menu Area. This menu (often referred to as the app's "hamburger" menu) will cause a menu of choices to become visible optionally allowing you to start a new game, buy "cheats", change the game's basic setup, clear stored state, etc. The menu can be dismissed with no selections by tapping any area outside of it. A tap on a label in the menu will invoke that option.
    The one option worth saying more about is "Set-up". If you select this option, you will be able to change the games 'difficulty'. The game is made more difficult by increasing the number of digits in the computer generatedTarget (and requires a corresponding change in the # of rows and columns in the guess input area) as well as increases in the range of digits that each position can be selected from. The other user-settable options should be self-explanatory. If something is changed that affects the play of a game (versus just an appearance or convenience option), the game will be restarted when you navigate back to the main game page.

Section B - the Guess Area. A digit of the guess will be entered when a number in the Selection Area is tapped. The "active" guess digit position is identified by the green outline of the rectangle in the guess  area. The green rectangle is automatically advanced after a guess position is filled by a tap on a selection number or can be manually selected by tapping a box. The automatic advancement can be forced to skip a location by double tapping the guess position's box to cause the "pad lock" image to show up. An additional tap will remove the "pad lock" image and the position is again a candidate for automatic advancement via tapped selections. Additionally, those that would prefer, are welcome to 'drag' a digit from the selection set and 'drop' it on to one of the guess locations. "Drag-n-drop" guessing does not affect the "active" guess location and can be combined with tapped selections as you see fit. You can also 'exchange' two guess positions by dragging one guess number on to another.

A number displayed in 'red' in the guess area is being flagged as being a repeated digit. Until this repeated digit problem is resolved, the 'Submit' button will not be active. Likewise, all red '-'s (Dashes) also must be replaced with a valid digit before the "Submit' button is activated.

Section C - the Selection Area. In this area, the set of digits that are available to guess with is displayed. When one of these numbers is tapped, whichever box in the guest area that is outlined in green will be filled with the tapped digit. Alternatively, as previously mentioned, a selection number can be 'dragged' and subequently 'dropped' onto a guess location. Numbers in this area are displayed with a light grey background just to show you that they have already been used at least once in the guess area. Additionally, background coloration is used to indicate numbers that have been "bought" (there is an optional way to buy 'hints' (aka cheats). The coloration will reflect the hint bought and will use the background color key shown in the header for section F, the Scratchpad area.

Note: There is an option available in the Setup submenu to have the Selection Area placed above the Guess Area versus the default (as shown) where it resides below it.

Section D - the Optional Display Area. This area can optionally display the Target the computer has selected for learning purposes. This option is selected via the menu of Section A.


Section E - Hint Purchasing Area. This area displays shortcuts for buying "hints" to help you guess the Target. Two types of hints are available.

The first lets you buy one of the potential numbers in the Selection Set and have it identified as being definitely not in (ie a Bagel) the Target  or conversely be identified as definitely in the Target (ie Not a Bagel ... meaning it is guaranteed to be a Pico or a Fermi when used in a guess).

The second type of hint you could buy is a Row Delta. A Row Delta hint will provide feedback as to how the sum of each digit in a row of the guess compares to the sum of the digits in the corresponding row of the Target. The difference in row sums will be displayed in Section I, the Feedback Area and will first show up as a magnitude (a +/- indication is shown to make it clear that the value shown only tells you the absolute difference between the row sums and not which sum is greater then or less than the other). Once sufficient 'Row Delta' hints have been bought to have all row's displaying the delta sum magnitude information, the next 'Row Delta' hint purchased will switch all rows to display the actual amount the guess row's sum would need to increase (a positive value) or decrease (a negative value) to equal the Target row's sum.


Section F - the Scratchpad Area. This are is provided strictly as a memory or thinking aid for the player. It has nothing to do with scoring or guessing. The numbers displayed can be tapped to cycle them through a series of colors that each can be associated with a meaning. The color key displayed directly above them is provided to show the meanings I have associated with the colors but in reality they really could be interpreted however you'd like. The one caveat is that when the "Buy a Number" hint is bought (identifying a number that is definitely a "bagel" or "not a bagel") the background of the corresponding number in the Scratchpad area turns either the orange 'bagel' color or the blue/green 'not-a-bagel' color.

Section G - the Scoring Area. The score is something that you are trying to maximize. The best score is thus the higest or closest to 100. The score is calculated as the :
score = 100 -  (the # of guesses) + (the number of minutes the game has been active for) + (the cost of any "hints" purchased)

An average scored is maintained and displayed for each "difficulty" level selectable from the games set-up page (accessible from the "hamburger menu" in Section A.



Section H - the Submit/New Game Button Area. This button is selected to 'submit' your guess for evaluation and feedback. Until the button is tapped, you are free to change your guess as many times as you'd like. After its selected, the guess will be assessed to determine if you have won i.e. your guess matches exactly the Target, and, if not a perfect match, how many picos, fermis, and bagels it contains. This information is conveyed in Section H, the Feedback Area. The "Submit' button will be deactivated until there is a valid guess in the Guess area. This means that the guess area contains only non-repeating numbers and no red '-' (Dashes).

Also, once a game is won, the 'Submit' button changes to a 'New Game' button to facilitate starting a new game. Alternatively, a new game can be selected from the "hamburger" menu in the upper right of the screen.

Section I - the Feedback Area. This scrollable area displays every guess that is made and the appropriate feedback on how that guess relates to the Target. Displayed information is the guess number, the guess itself, and the indication of the number of picos, fermis, and bagels the guess contains. Optionally, based on an appropriate purchase of 'Row Delta' hints, the feedback as to how the sum of each digit in a row of the guess compares to the sum of the digits in the corresponding row of the Target can be displayed.
